Destination

Palawan is a tropical paradise located in the western part of the Philippines. Known for its crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and diverse marine life, Palawan is a perfect destination for those who seek relaxation, adventure, and natural beauty. With its many stunning beaches, hidden lagoons, and lush forests, Palawan offers endless opportunities for outdoor activities such as island hopping, scuba diving, and trekking. Whether you're looking for a romantic getaway or an adventurous trip with friends and family, Palawan has something for everyone to enjoy.

So, whether you're a nature lover, adventure seeker, or just looking for a serene tropical escape, Palawan has got you covered. So pack your bags and get ready to experience the beauty of Palawan, here are some destinations you should visit in Palawan:

Underground River

The Underground River in Palawan, also known as the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. This incredible underground river system stretches over 8.2 km, winding its way through a stunning cave that boasts a unique landscape of spectacular rock formations and towering limestone cliffs. As you glide along the crystal-clear water on a guided boat tour, you'll be amazed by the awe-inspiring stalactites and stalagmites that have formed over millions of years.

Visiting the Underground River is a once-in-a-lifetime experience that you won't want to miss. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this breathtaking natural wonder. And if you're looking for the best way to experience the Underground River, be sure to book your guided tour in advance to ensure that you don't miss out on this unforgettable adventure.

El Nido

El Nido is a stunning destination in Palawan, known for its pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and towering limestone cliffs. Visitors can enjoy island hopping tours, snorkeling, and diving, where they can discover diverse marine life, hidden lagoons, and gorgeous coral reefs. This paradise is perfect for adventurers and beach enthusiasts who want to experience the beauty of Palawan's natural wonders.

With its pristine beaches, limestone cliffs, and crystal-clear waters, El Nido is a must-visit destination in Palawan. It offers a range of activities, including island-hopping tours, kayaking, diving, and snorkeling. You can also go on a hike and see the stunning views of the surrounding islands. There are plenty of accommodations in El Nido, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts.

Coron

Coron Island is a breathtaking destination that is famous for its turquoise waters, diverse marine life, and incredible dive sites. Visitors can explore the stunning Kayangan Lake, Twin Lagoon, and Barracuda Lake, where they can swim in crystal-clear waters and discover unique rock formations. Coron Island is a must-visit destination for those seeking adventure and natural beauty.

Coron is another popular destination in Palawan known for its stunning lagoons, coral reefs, and shipwrecks. It is perfect for those who love to explore the underwater world as it offers some of the best diving spots in the Philippines. Aside from diving, you can also go island hopping, visit the Maquinit Hot Springs, and take a dip in the Kayangan Lake, which is said to be the cleanest lake in the country.

Port Barton

Port Barton is a quiet fishing village that has recently become a popular destination for travelers seeking a more relaxed atmosphere. This charming town boasts a stunning beach with clear waters and pristine sand, perfect for swimming and sunbathing. Visitors can also take island hopping tours and discover beautiful marine life, hidden lagoons, and stunning waterfalls.

Port Barton is a quiet and laid-back town in Palawan, perfect for those who want to escape the crowds and relax in a peaceful setting. The town boasts a long stretch of white sand beach and crystal-clear waters, perfect for swimming, kayaking, and snorkeling. You can also go on a boat tour and visit nearby islands and beaches, or go on a hike and see the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Tubbataha Reef

Tubbataha Reef is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most beautiful dive sites in the world. This incredible marine sanctuary boasts diverse marine life, including over 600 species of fish, sharks, and sea turtles. Visitors can take a dive or snorkeling tour and explore the vibrant coral reefs and underwater caves. Tubbataha Reef is a must-visit destination for divers and snorkelers who want to experience Palawan's incredible marine life.

Tubbataha Reef is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the middle of the Sulu Sea. It is one of the best diving spots in the world, with a rich marine life that includes more than 600 species of fish, 360 species of coral, and several species of sharks, dolphins, and turtles. The reef is only accessible via live-aboard boats, which offer a unique and unforgettable diving experience.

Calauit Safari Park

Another tourist spot in Palawan that's worth a visit is the Calauit Safari Park. Located in Busuanga Island, the park is home to various African wildlife species such as giraffes, zebras, and antelopes. The park was established in the 1970s as a wildlife sanctuary for endangered African animals, and it has since become a popular tourist attraction.

Visitors can enjoy a safari tour around the park to observe the animals in their natural habitat. The park also offers other activities such as camping, trekking, and snorkeling. With its unique blend of African and Filipino wildlife, the Calauit Safari Park is definitely a must-visit destination for animal lovers and adventure seekers alike.
